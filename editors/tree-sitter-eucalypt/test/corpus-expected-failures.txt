## Gradual-typing syntax (0.10-0.12) — audit report §2.2. Mostly fixed in
## eu-2sa6.17 item 4: `:for`/`:let`/`:io` binding-scope keywords needed NO
## new grammar rule at all — they are ordinary block metadata symbols
## (`{ :for x: 42 }` already parsed as a block with a symbol-valued
## block_metadata `:for` followed by a normal declaration `x: 42`). The
## actual gap was `unit_metadata` not permitting trailing soup content
## after a leading top-level block (needed for block-dot access, e.g.
## `{ :for x: 42 }.(x)`) — fixed with a scoped `block, then optional
## trailing soup, gated by _declaration_end` rule (kept narrow: a fully
## general "unit_metadata = any soup" rule was tried and reverted — it
## created a genuine ambiguity with `repeat($.declaration)` that silently
## misparsed ~14 ordinary files, e.g. `f(x): x` got swallowed as a 2-element
## soup instead of a declaration). 034/035/037's real cause turned out to
## be unrelated to binding-scope keywords: a literal-string type-DSL idiom
## that catenates strings via a bare `\` used AS AN OPERATOR (e.g.
## `"\"a\" \ \"b\""`), and grammar.js's OPER_CHARS was simply missing `\`
## — fixed by adding it.
##

## lib/prelude.eu (the shipped stdlib) does not parse at all: hits the
## bracket-expr gap above (⌈[x]⌉/⌊[x]⌋, §2.3) plus nested-brace s"..."
## type-literal strings (§2.7, e.g. s"string → IO({..})"). The
## operator-prefix-collision bug formerly listed here (`<` then `<=` in the
## same scope) was fixed in eu-2sa6.17 item 3, root-caused as TWO bugs in
## the external scanner's declaration-boundary lookahead
## (src/scanner.c's at_declaration_start()), neither actually about
## "prefixes": (1) a missing immediate-adjacency check let a bare
## identifier that's the VALUE of one declaration be misparsed as the
## HEAD of the next declaration when that next declaration starts with
## `(`; (2) is_operator_char() was a third, independently-drifted copy of
## the operator-character set (grammar.js's OPER_CHARS and the Rust
## lexer's dynamic categories being the other two — see eu-fbyk), missing
## 14 characters (¬ ¡ £ € ± × ÷ ✓ ▶ △ ▽ ⨈ ? $), which made the scanner fail
## to recognise `(op ...):` declaration heads using them.
lib/prelude.eu
